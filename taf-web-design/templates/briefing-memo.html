Briefing Memo

Counseling moves outcomes in maternal care

To: Board, Investment Committee
From: Research Team
Date: March 2026
Subject: Maternal-Health Cohort Evaluation

A twelve-month evaluation across three TAF-backed organizations finds that household-level caregiver training — not just patient counseling — is the lever for durable behavior change.

Three findings
01

Counseling adherence rose 34%

Households with a trained family caregiver completed a full counseling sequence at a substantially higher rate than the control group, with the largest effect where the trained caregiver was a senior household member.

02

Postnatal visits up 21%

Trained caregivers reported faster recognition of warning signs, leading to earlier postnatal visits across all three sites.

03

Sustained at 12 months

Behavior change persisted at the 12-month follow-up, suggesting durable household-level practice change rather than a short-term study effect.

Recommendation

Direct the next round toward family-caregiver training

We recommend committing an additional $4M over two years to extend household-caregiver training across the cohort and to fund one external replication study with an independent research partner.
